PROJECT

My journey with HTML started with a simple desire: to build a webpage from scratch. I began by learning the basics—tags like html, head, body, and p. I experimented with creating headings, paragraphs, and links, watching my text come alive in a browser. As I got more confident, I tried building small projects. I created a personal profile page, complete with images using img and links with a. Learning about div and span helped me organize content, while table and form made my pages interactive. The real fun began when I added CSS to style my pages, turning plain text into visually appealing designs. I made mock websites, practiced structuring layouts, and even designed a portfolio to showcase my progress. It wasn’t just about writing code—it was about creativity and problem-solving. HTML opened the door to web development, and every small project taught me something new. Today, I look at every webpage with admiration, knowing I’ve learned the building blocks behind it.
